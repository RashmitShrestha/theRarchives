intellectus XIII

Purpose is more of a feeling than an actual thing. You can feel a sense of purpose without having a goal you're intentionally trying to reach. Like, chances are you felt like this as a little kid. You would go to school, listen to the teacher, do your homework, and play with friends without ever questioning why. You did those things simply because it seemed right for you to do those things. But were any of those things your life's purpose? Naw.

So to me, chasing purpose is like chasing happiness. Happiness occurs when you're too distracted by the goodness of life to spend a lot of time wondering how you can be happy. A sense of purpose occurs when you're too busy being engrossed in stuff to spend a lot of time wondering what your purpose is.

How do you develop a sense of purpose? You gotta find activities, people, or places that you love, where you feel like you belong. And if you don't love anything, you gotta figure out why you're so depressed and try to address that.

Why is a sense of purpose important? It is what keeps despair and hopelessness away.

Epikus XLVIII

I mean that there are many moral systems but one can disagree with them with an appeal to their own logic or moral inclinations (but also one's objections would be in reference to their objective findings around them). Christian presuppositionalists will say humans have no bases for their morals and logic without an external, omniscient reference but the problem with that is the ideas God espouses are all attempting to appeal to human moral reasoning and logic ie. if you obey god, you go to heaven which appeals to human desire, god is worthy of worship since he saved the jews from egypt, the bible all follows human dialogue structures, etc. This means the bible is implicitly validating human intellect and so is liable to criticisms based on human intellect. An alternative theory is epistemic nihilism, human logic can't be justified. If that were the case however, in order to establish the idea of nihilism itself as a real idea and coherent one must appeal to human logic ie. knowledge exists outside an individual's knowledge set is based on past human experience, the sentence itself implicitly validates human sentence structure logic, etc. Therefore, the idea of nihilism is an attempt to use human logic to subvert human logic. You run into the same inescapable tautology in the logical and moral question: correct logic is correct because it simply is by necessity, and our moral inclinations, if satisfying humans in a way that allows everyone to keep playing the game of life unhindered as possible, are correct by necessity.
