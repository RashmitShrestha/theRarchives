HYPNOS I

Dreams are an extension of our concious and emotional state that runs wild, especially during REM sleep. It's all in the mind therefore it's in your purview to control it. maybe not fully but regardless you do have a say in what you want to see in your dreams and how u want to feel. There are already techniques for lucid dreaming, so there's of course going to be ones for controlling the subject of dreams.

Whether you hate someone to death or love them to life, there's always some sort of emotional conneection that you make with them. And depending on the intensity of this connection, it might feel impossible for them to leave your thoughts. But in actuality, it's all in your head :)

However, if your connection with them is based on something unfinished, a negative problem that you believe can be mended, then there are a few things you can do.

  1. Since dreams are based on reality (or at least the reality perceived by your own bodily senses) there's something you have to change in that reality, which will then in turn change the way you perceive the person(s)/situation.
  2. If changing reality doesn't work, change the filter you use to perceive these ideas. The things you perceive using your senses all end up to your brain to analyze and utilize. But before this, it passes through this filter imposed directly by your thoughts. For example, try perceiving every new or old piece of information as a lie; try to demonize everything about that person; make any thoughts of them unnatractive to you. Or if your problems lie in another direction, try to justify their actions or pass them off as not in control. You can ultimately apply this filter to anything that you perceive. But of course, it's difficult.
  3. Dreaming itself is based on REM sleep, so if you want to change the way you perceive dreams, you have to change the sleep itself. Try making yourself more tired, both mentally and physically. Solve a puzzle, learn new math, do push ups, meditate, etc. Don't give your brain a chance to replay your subconcious while you're sleeping. Rather, let it focus on regaining energy and processing newly perceived information (math formulas, how to solve the puzzle, etc.)
If there are people in your life who constantly show up in your dreams, then it could be because they play an important role in your life that you haven’t (yet) fully acknowledged.

Make time for yourself to think about these people, and what they might represent to you — why do you think they’re showing up in your dreams all of a sudden?

if the person you're dreaming about is based on love (friendship, romantic, sexual, etc.), then it's because of the deep feelings you have for them, even if you don't fully accept it or choose to believe it.

This power is in your hands, even if it doesn't feel like it. You may not know what you want, and that's ok. However, the feeling that you get from this impurity, that is disconnected from not knowing what you want.

intellectus XIII

Purpose is more of a feeling than an actual thing. You can feel a sense of purpose without having a goal you're intentionally trying to reach. Like, chances are you felt like this as a little kid. You would go to school, listen to the teacher, do your homework, and play with friends without ever questioning why. You did those things simply because it seemed right for you to do those things. But were any of those things your life's purpose? Naw.

So to me, chasing purpose is like chasing happiness. Happiness occurs when you're too distracted by the goodness of life to spend a lot of time wondering how you can be happy. A sense of purpose occurs when you're too busy being engrossed in stuff to spend a lot of time wondering what your purpose is.

How do you develop a sense of purpose? You gotta find activities, people, or places that you love, where you feel like you belong. And if you don't love anything, you gotta figure out why you're so depressed and try to address that.

Why is a sense of purpose important? It is what keeps despair and hopelessness away.

Time is relative to the observer. When a person moving closer to the speed of light time moves slower from them. Like an astronaut going to Alpha Proxima at near light speed time moves about over four years. Time on Earth would have surged ahead by a century. Time is part of the Space-time fabric.

We live in a universe with three spatial dimensions and one time dimension. So we experience space like up and down and side to side and so forth. We experience time moving forward.
